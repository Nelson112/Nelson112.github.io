Historic places in the V&A waterfront

Clock Tower
The V&A Waterfront Clock Tower is situated
in Table Bay Harbour, close to the site of
the original Bertie's Landing restaurant. Much
of the Waterfront's charm lies in the fact
that this busy commercial harbour is set in the
midst of a huge entertainment venue with restaurants,
specialty shops, craft markets, theatres and movies.
Swing Bridge
Walking to the pier head to stop over at the
Clock Tower precinct or to board the Robben Island
ferry brings you to the pedestrian swing bridge, an
innovative bridge designed to swing over the waterway to
allow shipping into and out of the Alfred Basin.
The bridge is automatic but
can be manually operated in the event of failure.
African Trading Port
The considerable amount of time that has been devoted to sourcing African art has allowed us to establish close relationships with successive generations of Africa’s leading artists. We stock an immense array of objects d’art including sculpture, artifacts, ceramics, pottery bronzeware and glassware. Although our collection focuses on the work of recognised artists, we also devote display space to some of the younger, emerging artists from the African continent.”
